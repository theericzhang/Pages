Ride Safe, Ride BART: Restoring ridership in the Post-Pandemic Bay Area [CONCEPT]
With the San Francisco Bay Area approaching city-wide reopening since the onset of COVID-19, its subway system BART required an experience redesign to regain ridership, build trust, and keep riders safe.
Overview
Since March 2020, San Francisco's BART had been hit by a 93% decrease in ridership in the Bay Area due by the COVID-19 pandemic. With its only relief issued as government stimulus funds, the mass transit provider would need to prioritize restoring ridership in a safe manner.

The digital redesign of BART's messaging in Google Maps and the launch of a public relations campaign greatly eased the anxiety around traveling with BART.

I was the lead Experience Designer on this project specializing in research, interaction design, and content strategy. I collaborated with two brilliant designers on this concept project, Noah Goldstein and Ale Hiraoka, in a two week long design sprint.
